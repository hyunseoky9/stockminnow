Simulation Instructions

Simulation Overview

Welcome to the Hatchery Augmentation Simulation! This is an interactive environment where you make decisions about fish hatchery operations to maintain healthy fish populations while managing environmental constraints.

Your Goal: Make strategic decisions about hatchery operations over multiple years to achieve the best possible outcomes for fish population management.

Key Features:

  • Multi-year simulation spanning several decades
  • Real-time feedback on your decisions
  • Dynamic population and environmental conditions
  • Reward system based on conservation objectives

Population and Hydrology Dynamics

The simulation models complex interactions between fish populations and environmental conditions:

Population Dynamics:

  • Natural recruitment varies based on environmental conditions
  • Population size affects survival rates and reproduction
  • Hatchery fish supplement wild populations

Hydrology Effects:

  • Water flow affects habitat quality and fish survival
  • Drought years create challenging conditions
  • Seasonal variations impact fish behavior and reproduction

These factors combine to create realistic scenarios that require adaptive management strategies.

Action Selection

Each year, you must choose how many fish to stock from the hatchery:

Action Range: You can stock between 0 and the maximum stocking capacity (varies by conditions)

Decision Considerations:

  • Current Population: Low populations may benefit from higher stocking
  • Environmental Conditions: Poor conditions may reduce stocking effectiveness
  • Resource Constraints: Hatchery capacity and budget limitations
  • Long-term Impact: Consider effects on future years

How to Act: Enter your stocking decision in the action input field and click "Take Action" to proceed to the next year.

Reward System

Your performance is evaluated based on how well you achieve conservation objectives:

Reward Components:

  • Population Health: Maintaining stable, viable fish populations
  • Efficiency: Achieving goals without excessive resource use
  • Sustainability: Long-term population viability
  • Adaptability: Responding appropriately to changing conditions

Scoring:

  • Positive rewards for successful population management
  • Penalties for population crashes or overuse of resources
  • Bonus rewards for exceptional performance

Your total reward across all years reflects your overall management effectiveness.
